### General Assessment

**1. Learning and Working Environment**

During my participation in the First Cloud Journey program, I had the opportunity to experience a dynamic, friendly, and highly practical learning environment. The program members were always willing to communicate, share materials, and provide support whenever I encountered difficulties during the learning process or project implementation.

Through the program’s activities, events, and practical sessions, I became more familiar with working according to a clear plan, proactively researching technical documents, and presenting work results in a more structured manner.

**2. Support from Mentors and the Admin Team**

The mentors and admin team supported me in defining my learning direction, providing reference materials, answering questions, and giving feedback throughout the project implementation process.

I highly appreciate that they not only provided instructions on how to complete tasks but also encouraged interns to conduct independent research, troubleshoot issues, and propose possible solutions. As a result, I was able to improve my proactiveness and problem-solving abilities instead of relying entirely on predefined instructions.

**3. Relevance of the Program to My Academic Major**

The program content was highly relevant to the Information Technology major that I am currently studying. My previous knowledge of programming, databases, computer networks, and application development helped me approach AWS services more effectively.

In addition, the program helped me broaden my knowledge in the following areas:

* Cloud computing.
* Serverless architecture.
* Access permission management.
* Data processing and storage.
* Web application deployment.
* Monitoring, security, and cost control on AWS.

**4. Opportunities for Learning and Skill Development**

During the internship, I had the opportunity to study and gain practical experience with various AWS services, including:

* AWS Lambda.
* Amazon DynamoDB.
* AWS IoT Core.
* Amazon API Gateway.
* Amazon Cognito.
* Amazon S3.
* AWS Amplify.
* AWS WAF.
* Amazon EventBridge.
* Amazon SNS.
* Amazon CloudWatch.

The implementation of the **Smart Home Energy Waste Monitoring and Alert System** project helped me gain a clearer understanding of how different AWS services can be integrated to form a complete system.

In addition to technical knowledge, I also improved the following skills:

* Searching for and reading technical documentation.
* Analyzing requirements.
* Designing system architecture.
* Testing and troubleshooting issues.
* Writing technical documents and reports.
* Managing work progress.
* Presenting implementation results.

**5. Culture and Teamwork**

Throughout the program, the members demonstrated a strong spirit of mutual support and knowledge sharing. When problems occurred during practical activities, participants could discuss them together to identify the causes and determine appropriate solutions.

I experienced a respectful working environment among the members and learned how to receive feedback and adjust my work based on the guidance and comments of mentors.

**6. Program Content and Organization**

The program provided a wide range of learning materials, laboratory exercises, workshops, and AWS-related events. These activities helped me gradually become familiar with AWS, from fundamental knowledge to the process of building a practical project.

However, the amount of knowledge covered by the program was relatively large. Therefore, interns needed to proactively manage their time, review the materials, and practise regularly to keep up with the program schedule.

### Suggestions and Expectations

* I hope the program will continue to organize more practical workshops on AWS architecture, security, DevOps, and artificial intelligence.
* Additional training sessions on the AWS Well-Architected Framework and cost optimization could be included.
* More knowledge-sharing activities between project groups could be organized so that interns can explore different implementation approaches.
* More sample documents could be provided to demonstrate how to write technical documentation and present system architecture.
* A final project presentation session could be organized so that each group can introduce its results and receive direct feedback.

### Future Expectations

Given the opportunity, I would like to continue participating in activities, workshops, or advanced programs organized by First Cloud Journey.

I also hope to continue learning and developing my knowledge in the following areas:

* Cloud Architecture.
* Serverless Applications.
* Cloud Security.
* DevOps and CI/CD.
* Containers and Kubernetes.
* Monitoring and Cost Optimization.

The First Cloud Journey program provided me with a stronger foundation in cloud computing, helped me better understand the process of deploying a system on AWS, and enabled me to identify the skills that I need to continue improving in the future.
